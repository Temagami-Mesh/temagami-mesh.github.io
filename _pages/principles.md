# Community Principles & Code of Conduct

As the Temagami Meshtastic project gains popularity, we want to ensure our community remains welcoming, collaborative, and focused on what matters most: building a reliable, open, and useful off-grid communication network for the Lake Temagami area.

These 10 principles guide our activities, decisions, and interactions. They draw from **open source** best practices, the spirit of the **Amateur Radio Code**, and the practical needs of a remote Northern Ontario lake community. They are designed to head off conflict, encourage experimentation, and keep the project sustainable and fun as we grow.

We treat this as both a technical project *and* a community of practice — part mesh network, part amateur radio club, part neighborhood resilience initiative.

## Our 10 Guiding Principles

### 1. Embrace Open Source & Transparency
Everything we create — firmware tweaks, node configurations, documentation, KML maps, integration code, weather bots, or hardware guides — is released openly. We use permissive or copyleft licenses (MIT, GPL, CC-BY-SA) as appropriate and give clear credit to contributors. Forks are welcome; coordination on core local compatibility is encouraged.

### 2. Actively Support Experimentation & Building
We explicitly celebrate and encourage members who want to tinker, prototype, and innovate. Custom antennas, solar-powered routers, sensor integrations (Ecowitt weather, lake level, etc.), Meshtastic + Meshtastic + LoRa experiments, or even Meshtastic + ham radio cross-band setups are all valued. Share your builds, failures, and discoveries — this is how the network and our collective skills advance.

### 3. Uphold the Amateur Radio Spirit
Even though much of our activity uses license-free ISM/LoRa bands, we operate in the spirit of the classic Amateur Radio Code:  
**Considerate** • **Loyal** • **Progressive** • **Friendly** • **Balanced** • **Community-oriented**.  
We strive for technical excellence, patient mentoring of newcomers, and operating practices that enhance (rather than diminish) the experience for everyone sharing the spectrum and the mesh.

### 4. Prioritize Local Community Benefit & Resilience
The primary purpose of Temagami Meshtastic is to serve the people who live, cottage, work, and visit on and around Lake Temagami. We prioritize emergency preparedness, reliable comms during outages or storms, and practical day-to-day uses that make island and remote life safer and more connected. Public-service mindset comes first.

### 5. Be Genuinely Inclusive & Accessible
We welcome participants of every skill level — from complete beginners who just want a simple node, to experienced radio amateurs and tinkerers. No gatekeeping, no condescension. We provide beginner-friendly onboarding, clear documentation, and patient answers. Diversity of perspectives strengthens the network.

### 6. Share Knowledge Generously & Document Everything
If you figure something out, write it down and share it. Good documentation, photos of installs, range-test results, configuration examples, and troubleshooting guides multiply the value for everyone. We maintain a living knowledge base (this site + GitHub wiki) so knowledge doesn’t stay siloed in private chats.

### 7. Operate Legally, Responsibly & Considerately
We comply fully with Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED) regulations. We use appropriate power levels, avoid harmful interference, and are mindful of other users of the band. When the mesh carries traffic, we respect that emergency or priority messages may need precedence. Good operators make the whole system better.

### 8. Respect Privacy, Property & the Environment
- Obtain explicit permission before placing any node or antenna on private property, shared docks, or structures.  
- Be thoughtful about what data is broadcast over the mesh.  
- Design deployments with low power draw and renewable energy (solar) in mind.  
- Leave no trace — respect the pristine beauty and ecology of the Temagami region.

### 9. Collaborate on Network Health & Thoughtful Growth
Adding nodes or changing settings affects everyone. We coordinate major router placements, channel plans, and firmware versions to keep the mesh reliable, redundant where needed, and performant. We favor quality and coverage over sheer quantity of nodes. Growth is welcomed but managed for the collective good.

### 10. Resolve Disagreements Respectfully & Keep It Enjoyable
We assume good intent. When conflicts or differing opinions arise (technical choices, node placement, project direction), we discuss them openly and constructively, focusing on evidence and the project’s long-term health. Personal attacks, harassment, or toxicity have no place here. At the end of the day, this is a hobby and a community — it should be rewarding and fun for everyone involved.

## How These Principles Work in Practice

- **New members**: We point newcomers to this page early so expectations are clear.
- **Decision making**: For core infrastructure (shared routers, primary channels, map curation), we aim for rough consensus among active contributors. Smaller experiments and personal nodes are fully autonomous.
- **Conflict escalation**: Most issues are resolved through respectful discussion in our community channels or GitHub. Persistent or serious problems can be brought to project maintainers for mediation.
- **Living document**: These principles can evolve. Suggestions for improvement or clarification are always welcome via GitHub issues or community discussion.

## Built on Open Source Foundations

Temagami Meshtastic is proudly built on the excellent open-source [Meshtastic](https://meshtastic.org) firmware, apps, and ecosystem. We contribute back where we can and encourage all members to support the upstream project as well.
